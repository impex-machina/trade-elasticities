# v0.2.0 -> v0.3.0: what changed and why

Companion note to the v0.3.0 release. Tables below are generated by
`analysis/compare_runs.R` (2026-07-08) from the two published Stage 1 and
Stage 2b tables; this header interprets them. The v0.2.0 baseline is pinned
at HF revision `7e598f6cb98e`.

v0.3.0 is a bug-fix release, not a methodology change: the estimator, data,
sample construction rules, tuning constants (shrinkage lambda, caps, trim),
and package stack (AMI R 4.5.3 library, identical to the v0.2.0 run) are all
held fixed. Six patches changed the numbers through three mechanisms: (1) a
prior-scale correction -- Stage 1 exported the bounded transform
omega/(1+omega) where Stage 2 consumes Soderbery's gamma (the inverse
export-supply elasticity) directly, so every Stage 2a shrinkage anchor,
plateau fallback, and starting value was systematically pulled toward zero;
(2) a corrected delta-method Jacobian for the Stage 1 (sigma, rho, omega)
standard errors, whose ported form was the elementwise-absolute transpose of
the true Jacobian; and (3) a set of honesty fixes to diagnostics -- a
minimum-eigenvalue Cragg-Donald weak-IV statistic in place of a trace form,
a consecutive-year guard on Stage 1 first differences, per-parameter cap
flags, and a metric-consistent Sargan statistic.

## The headline: the supply side re-levels onto Soderbery's benchmarks

The demand side barely moves (sigma median 2.875 -> 2.878; provenance and
floor shares shift by fractions of a point from the 7,753 cells the gap
guard removes). The supply side re-levels: gamma's median rises from 0.238
to 0.680, and the run's structural ratios now sit on Soderbery (2018)
Table 2 almost exactly -- gamma/(1+gamma) = 0.405 vs his 0.408, 1/(sigma-1)
= 0.532 vs 0.532, gamma/((1+gamma)(sigma-1)) = 0.204 vs 0.217. v0.2.0
missed the first ratio by a third. The prior-scale bug was the thing
standing between this pipeline and the published literature, and its
removal is externally validated by that convergence.

The substantive reinterpretation for consumers: the implied median export-
supply elasticity falls from 4.2 (v0.2.0, inflated by the downward-biased
gamma) to 1.47 -- from "quite elastic" to "moderately inelastic" -- and the
median implied optimal tariff roughly doubles (0.35 -> 0.68). Any analysis
that consumed v0.2.0 gamma, opt_tariff, or the implied elasticity should
re-pull; do not mix versions within one exercise.

## Standard errors and screens: more honest, mechanically validated

sigma_se rises ~38% at the median (0.995 -> 1.370) and rho_se falls ~66%
(0.586 -> 0.200), the directions and rough magnitudes the corrected
Jacobian implies; omega_se drops ~3x. The per-parameter cap flags make the
accounting exact for the first time: `adjust = 4` now coincides with the
sigma-at-cap share to the decimal (13.5%), an omega-only cap is 3.4%, and
the previously invisible double-capped cells surface at ~0.6% of ok cells.
Downstream, the sigma_robust screen passes 10.6% of rows (was 16.5%): of
the two opposing forces -- larger corrected sigma_se failing more pole
tests vs omega-only-capped cells becoming eligible -- the first dominates.
Fewer cells now claim sigma-robustness because the old claim rested on
understated standard errors.

The weak-IV screen is the largest single swing: the median first-stage
statistic halves (4.50 -> 2.08) and the Stock-Yogo pass share falls from
59.3% to 17.3%. Nothing about identification changed -- the trace-form
statistic averaged a strong direction over a weak one, and the
minimum-eigenvalue Cragg-Donald form (the statistic the Stock-Yogo critical
values are actually tabulated for) does not. On the bit-identical synthetic
pillar rerun (same seeds, unchanged point-estimate path), med_fstat falls
3.3x with all point estimates, biases, and yields unchanged -- a clean A/B
isolating the diagnostic. HS4 bilateral panels are mostly weakly
identified; v0.3.0 says so.

One unforced improvement cuts against the everything-got-stricter reading:
the share of rows with a clean cell-specific gamma SE rises from 58.7% to
64.9%, with boundary and non-converged fits roughly halved. Scale-
consistent priors and starting values improved optimizer health, not just
levels. Relative precision improves too: median gamma_se/gamma falls from
~1.4 to ~0.95 -- though the README's caution that the SE is frequently as
large as the estimate still stands.

## What validates the SE fix -- and what does not

The Jacobian correction is validated by (a) machine-precision agreement
with numerical differentiation of the structural inversion and of the
forward map, and (b) the Pillar 3 Monte Carlo, where penalized Gauss-Newton
gamma SEs sit within -1.8% to +6.7% of truth across all four regimes.
Tier 1a confidence-interval coverage is NOT a validation channel here and
did not move: coverage in that grid is bound by conditional point-estimate
bias (best where bias is smallest, collapsing to ~0.42 where median bias
reaches -75%), and an interval centered 30-75% from truth cannot cover at
95% however correctly its width is computed. Selection (roughly one-third
estimation yield) compounds this. The coverage table measures the
estimator's small-sample bias under weak identification -- a documented
limitation -- not the SE formula.

## Bookkeeping

Stage 1 ok cells fall 149,577 -> 141,824 (-5.2%): the gap guard nulls
first differences spanning non-consecutive years, removing contaminated
moments concentrated in thin panels. Stage 2b rows rise 6,809,496 ->
6,831,781 (+0.3%) purely from the 0.5%-per-tail trim cutting at new
quantiles. `adjust` semantics changed (code 4 = sigma capped regardless of
omega; code 5 = omega-only), so cross-version comparisons of codes 4/5
should use the new `sigma_capped` / `omega_capped` booleans. The run used
the AMI system library with the renv autoloader disabled -- deliberately,
holding package versions identical to v0.2.0 so version drift is excluded
as a confounder in every delta above.
